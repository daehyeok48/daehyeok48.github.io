Structural and Dynamical Crossovers in Dense Electrolytes

Daehyeok Kim1, Taejin Kwon2,*, and Jeongmin Kim3,*

February 3, 2026 · Physical Review E

1Department of Energy Engineering, Korea Institute of Energy Technology (KENTECH), Naju 58330, Republic of Korea
2Department of Chemistry and Cosmetics, Jeju National University, Jeju 63243, Republic of Korea
3Department of Chemistry Education and Graduate Department of Chemical Materials, Pusan National University, Busan 46241, Republic of Korea

Overview
This study uses molecular dynamics simulations of primitive electrolyte models to investigate how structural and dynamical correlations evolve with salt concentration. By comparing systems with explicit space-filling solvent particles and implicit-solvent models, the work identifies electrostatic screening crossovers accompanied by changes in ion self-diffusion and ion-pair lifetimes.

The results show that ion-solvent coupling changes the nature of the screening crossover, while the onset of underscreening is associated mainly with local ion pairing or small ionic aggregates rather than a percolating ionic network. The diffusion-corrected ion-pair lifetime is used as a descriptor linking short-range ionic structure with transport dynamics across the model systems.
