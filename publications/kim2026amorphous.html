Amorphous π-Conjugated Passivator Facilitates Widening Process Window for Reliable Perovskite Solar Modules

Overview
This work reports an amorphous π-conjugated passivator, I-4PACz, for interfacial control in perovskite solar cells and modules. The asymmetric iodine-substituted carbazole design enhances the molecular dipole moment, suppresses ordered molecular packing, and enables the formation of a uniform interfacial layer.

Devices and modules using I-4PACz show improved charge extraction, broader tolerance to passivator concentration and blade-coating speed, and reliable module performance. The reported perovskite solar modules reached 21.2% power conversion efficiency with a 24.5 cm2 aperture area, with stability tests showing retention under thermal stress and continuous illumination.
